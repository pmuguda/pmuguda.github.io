About Me

I'm a Remote Sensing Data Scientist at Space42 in Abu Dhabi and a PhD researcher at TU Wien, Department of Geodesy and Geoinformation. I hold a Master's in Earth Oriented Space Science & Technology (ESPACE) from TU Munich with specialisation in Remote Sensing.

My work spans the full SAR processing chain — from raw SLC co-registration and InSAR deformation monitoring to operational Copernicus soil moisture products fusing Sentinel-1 and ASCAT at sub-kilometre resolution.

Previously at DLR (German Aerospace Center), TU Munich, and Universität der Bundeswehr München, always at the intersection of radar signal processing, deep learning, and geospatial intelligence.

Featured Projects

SCATSAR Soil Water Index Pipeline (Copernicus)
Near-real-time fusion of Sentinel-1 SAR and ASCAT surface soil moisture for Copernicus CGLOPS. Produces Soil Water Index at 0.5 km resolution across Europe using recursive exponential filter formulations.
PythonSentinel-1 ASCATNRT PipelineCopernicus
InSAR Denoising via Self-Supervised Learning
Complex-valued U-Net with non-local attention blocks for InSAR phase denoising. Includes SAR interferogram simulation pipelines and benchmarking against state-of-the-art filters on real Sentinel-1 data.
PyTorchInSAR Self-SupervisedU-NetSentinel-1
Drought Monitoring – ASCAT over East Africa
Operational drought indicator workflow for MetOp ASCAT SSM at 6.25 km, covering both climate data records and near-real-time applications for humanitarian early warning over East Africa (ROSSIHNI project).

More intense Indian summer monsoons
Satellite data from 1991 to 2020 reveal heavier summer rainfall that is likely to continue. Analysis shows daily rainfall increased by 0.1 mm per decade across India, driven by accelerating temperature contrasts between the warming Himalayas and the Indian Ocean.
